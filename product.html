The World of Production

Displaying a list of products on your site is one thing. Displaying products and letting people transfer money electronically is a totally different thing and requires extra setup. To get paid online you typically need four things; A website, a payment gateway, a merchant account, and a Bank account for your business. The website is fairly obvious, this is where the customer will add products to their cart, they will check out, and they will enter credit card information. The website needs to send that information to the payment gateway. The payment gateway checks to make sure that funds are available. If funds are not available the payment gateway will return that information to the website.

The website can then display an error to the customer. If funds are available then the payment gateway will return that information to the website to complete the transaction. The next step depends on what type of payment gateway you have.

There are two broad categories; Traditional payment gateways and modern payment gateways. A traditional gateway you need a merchant account, once a payment has been authorized by the gateway it is the merchant account that draws the funds, holds onto them for a few days and then depositsthem into your business bank account. In modern gateway combines the payment gateway functionality w/ the merchant account so you don’t need to have a separate merchant account. The gateway itself verifies the funds and draws them. It holds on to the funds for a few days and then deposits them into your business bank account just like traditional gateway.If you don’t know which payment gateway to use you should start with a modern payment gateway so you don’t have to figure out how to get that merchant account.

The only exception is if you have a brick and mortar store bc you will already need a merchant account for your in-store credit card processing. There are price differences between the different gateways but they are not worth worrying about until you start pulling in serious revenue. Anything involving credit cards requires extra security. Many gateways give you the option to authorize or to authorize and capture. Authorizing means you verify that you can make the charge if you want.If someone enters an incorrect credit card number it will be caught at this step. Capturing is when you actually transfer the funds. You can not capture w/out authorizing at the same time or having a previous authorization. You are only suppose to capture a charge when you ship the item. Capturing a charge can be done from w/in the payment gateway.
